Project Overview

Current Situation

GoFit Toa Payoh - Your Foundation

  • Membership-driven model with 400+ contracts
  • PT packages generating 2x membership revenue (strong demand for personalized service)
  • 3 years operational, profitable with healthy margins
  • 24/7 self-service positioning

New Venture - Key Differentiators

  • Location: Siglap/East Coast (affluent, expat-heavy, family-oriented demographic)
  • Space: 1,600 sqft with shower room at 77 @ East Coast condominium
  • Concept: Strength training + physiotherapy fusion
  • Focus: Premium 1-to-1 and semi-private training
  • Target: Maintenance, elevation, rehabilitation for affluent individuals

Strategic Positioning

Premium strength and recovery studio - not a traditional gym. The combination of strength training and physiotherapy occupies valuable middle ground where performance meets rehabilitation.

Positioning Statement

"Where strength meets science" - A specialized training and recovery studio that combines evidence-based strength programming with physiotherapy expertise to help established professionals and active adults train smarter, recover faster, and build lasting resilience.

Proposed Brand Strategy

Target Customer Profile

  • Age: 35-60 years old
  • Demographics: Affluent professionals and families in East Coast corridor
  • Psychographics:
    • Previous gym-goers dealing with injuries or age-related concerns
    • Athletes/active individuals needing rehabilitation
    • Time-poor individuals willing to pay premium for results
    • Preventative health-conscious segment

Revenue Model Recommendations

Given GoFit's experience where PT packages outperform memberships 2:1:

  1. Primary Revenue: Semi-Private Training Sessions (2-4 people) - Higher margin than 1-to-1, builds community
  2. Premium Revenue: 1-to-1 Training + Physio Packages - Flagship offering
  3. Foundation Revenue: Limited Membership Access - 50-100 members for off-peak hours
  4. Ancillary: Assessment & Programming - Initial consultations, movement assessments

Operational Model

  • Hours: Appointment-based (not 24/7)
  • Peak times: 6-9am, 12-2pm, 6-9pm
  • Staffing: Dual-credentialed trainers (strength + physio background) or partnerships with physios
  • Capacity: 6-8 training slots per day max to maintain premium positioning

Key Brand Pillars

Expertise

Qualified physios and specialized strength coaches delivering evidence-based programming.

Personalization

No cookie-cutter programs. Every plan tailored to individual bodies and goals.

Longevity

Training for life, not just aesthetics. Building resilience that lasts decades.

Science-backed

Evidence-based programming and recovery protocols, not fitness trends.

Pricing Strategy

Premium positioning 30-50% above standard PT rates in Singapore. The physio integration justifies higher pricing and attracts clients who view this as healthcare investment, not gym expense.

Brand Name Options

KINETIC Assessment Summary

Legal Status: No direct trademark conflict in Singapore IPOS registry. Kinetics Climbing Gym exists but in different category. Kinetic X Fitness permanently closed.

Strategic Concerns: Heavily saturated globally (20+ fitness/physio businesses). Requires more SEO investment. Alternative spellings (KINETIK, KYNETIC) NOT recommended due to spelling confusion, unprofessional perception, and SEO issues.

Verdict: Viable with strong differentiator (e.g., "KINETIC Strength Studio"), but more distinctive names offer easier market positioning.

Tier 1 - Recommended Names

FORGE Strength Studio TOP CHOICE

"Forged for Life"

Rationale: "Forge" evokes craftsmanship, resilience, rebuilding - perfect for rehab + strength combo. Suggests transformation through deliberate, expert work.

Pros
  • Strong brand personality
  • Differentiates from clinical feel
  • Unique in Singapore
  • Premium craft associations
  • Clean SEO opportunity
Cons
  • Doesn't explicitly mention physio
  • May feel masculine-leaning

KINETIC Strength Studio

"Movement. Mastered."

Rationale: Relates to movement science, bridges athletic and clinical. Scientific credibility with professional warmth.

Pros
  • Scientific credibility
  • Clear movement focus
  • Professional feel
  • No trademark conflict
Cons
  • Globally saturated
  • Higher SEO competition
  • Local climbing gym confusion
  • Requires bigger marketing budget

APEX Performance & Rehab

"Peak Strength. Lasting Recovery."

Rationale: "Apex" signals peak performance and premium positioning. Dual naming clearly communicates both services.

Pros
  • Clear service indication
  • Aspirational positioning
  • Professional credibility
  • Works well verbally
Cons
  • "Apex" somewhat common
  • Longer name

RESILIENCE Strength & Physio

"Build Strength That Lasts"

Rationale: Speaks directly to target - people rebuilding, maintaining, preventing decline. Emotionally resonant for 40+ demographic.

Pros
  • Clear dual offering
  • Emotional connection
  • Premium feel
  • Longevity positioning
Cons
  • Longer name
  • Slightly medical sounding

Launch Marketing Plan - East Coast Market

Phase 1: Pre-Launch (8-10 weeks before opening)

Week 1-3: Foundation Building

1. Brand Identity Rollout

  • Logo, color palette, brand guidelines finalized
  • Website development begins (live 4 weeks pre-launch)
  • Social media handles secured (Instagram, Facebook)
  • Google Business Profile created

2. Founding Member Program

  • Create exclusive "Founding Member" offer: 50 spots only
  • Benefits: Locked-in rates for 12 months, priority booking, complimentary movement assessment ($200 value)
  • Pricing: 20% discount on package rates for first 50 sign-ups
  • Goal: Secure 30-40 commitments before opening, generate pre-launch revenue

3. Strategic Partnerships

  • East Coast orthopedic/sports medicine clinics (referral agreements)
  • Siglap/Marine Parade family doctors
  • Local sports clubs (cycling, running, tennis)
  • Yoga/Pilates studios (complementary services)
  • Premium lifestyle businesses (cafes, wellness centers)

Week 4-6: Awareness Generation

1. Hyper-Local Digital Targeting

  • Facebook/Instagram Ads: Geo-targeted within 3km radius of Siglap
  • Audience: Ages 35-60, interests in fitness/health/wellness
  • Budget: $1,500-2,000/month
  • Creative: Brand story, founder story, facility sneak peeks
  • Lead magnet: Free movement assessment - capture 100+ leads

2. Content Marketing Launch

Instagram content pillars:

  • Education: "Common strength mistakes after 40"
  • Transformation: "Rebuilding after injury" stories
  • Behind-the-scenes: Facility build-out, equipment arrival
  • Expert tips: Short-form physio/training advice
  • Post frequency: 4-5x per week
  • Reels/video content: 2-3x per week

3. PR & Media Outreach

  • Press release: Straits Times, 8Days Singapore, Honey Combers
  • Angle: "New hybrid strength-physio studio brings personalized approach to East Coast"
  • Pitch lifestyle/wellness writers for feature stories

4. Community Engagement

  • Sponsor local East Coast events (parkrun, community activities)
  • Attendance at East Coast condo events/markets
  • Coffee shop partnerships for leaflet distribution

Week 7-8: Conversion Push

1. Open House Events (2 weekends before launch)

  • Saturday/Sunday afternoon sessions
  • Free mini-assessments (15-min movement screens)
  • Facility tours
  • Meet the trainers/physios
  • Special opening week offers for attendees
  • Goal: 100+ visitors, 20-30 package sales

2. Influencer/Ambassador Seeding

  • Identify 5-10 micro-influencers in East Coast (3k-15k followers)
  • Offer complimentary training packages for authentic content
  • Focus: Parents, fitness enthusiasts, professionals 35-55
  • Not just "fitness influencers" - lifestyle, wellness, local personalities

3. Email Marketing Campaign

  • Weekly emails to growing lead list
  • Content: Educational tips, opening updates, exclusive offers
  • Countdown sequence in final 2 weeks

Phase 2: Grand Opening (Week of Launch)

Opening Week Strategy

1. Soft Opening (First 3 days - Wed/Thu/Fri)

  • Founding members only
  • Iron out operational kinks
  • Capture testimonial content

2. Official Launch (Saturday)

  • Grand opening event: 10am-4pm
  • Free assessments, facility tours, live demonstrations
  • Local partnerships present (referral booths)
  • Media attendance
  • Special launch packages (48-hour flash sale)

3. Opening Week Promotion

  • "Launch Week Special": 15% off all packages booked within 7 days
  • Daily social media content: client experiences, facility showcase
  • Google Ads launch targeting "physiotherapy Siglap", "personal training East Coast"

Phase 3: Post-Launch (Month 2-3)

Month 2: Momentum Building

  • Referral Program: "Refer a friend, both get 1 free session" - Limited to first 50 referrals
  • Corporate Partnerships: Target CBD/Marina Bay companies, lunch-time training slots
  • Content Expansion: Client success stories (video), educational workshops, Google Business reviews
  • Paid Advertising: Analyze first month data, shift budget to best-performing channels, retargeting campaigns

Month 3: Community & Retention

  • Member Events: Monthly community workout or wellness workshop
  • Partnership Activation: Joint events with clinic partners, cross-promotional campaigns
  • PR Push Round 2: Success stories for media, "3 Months In" update features
