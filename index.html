Environmental/Space Design

Studio Aesthetic

Overall Feel: Scandinavian Wellness meets Performance Lab. Natural materials with biophilic design - warm wood tones, living greenery, abundant natural light. Premium residential spa aesthetic rather than industrial warehouse. Clean, serene, sophisticated.

Core Design Principles

  • Natural Materials First: Light wood (oak, ash) as primary material - warm, inviting, premium
  • Biophilic Integration: Living walls, plants, maximum natural light - wellness through nature
  • Equipment as Sculpture: Black equipment contrasts beautifully against light wood - functional art
  • Spa-like Serenity: Calm, clean, organized - luxury wellness club, not gym
  • Lighting Layers: Natural light priority, warm ambient lighting, strategic accent lighting

Material Palette

Material Application Effect
Light Wood Slats Feature walls, equipment storage integration, ceiling details Warmth, texture, premium residential feel, sound dampening
Light Wood Flooring Training floor, throughout main spaces Cohesive, warm, high-end, easier on joints than concrete
Clean White Ceilings, walls (where not wood), reception desk Brightness, openness, clinical precision, spa-like calm
Black Metal/Equipment All training equipment, racks, storage systems Strong contrast, professional, sculptural quality
Living Greenery Moss walls (backlit), potted plants, vertical gardens Biophilic wellness, air quality, visual calm, Instagram-worthy
Glass Mirrors, consultation room partitions, equipment storage Openness, light reflection, space expansion

Lighting Strategy

  • Natural Light: Maximum window exposure, large glass panels where possible
  • Ambient Lighting: Recessed cove lighting in ceiling, warm white (3000K), even distribution
  • Accent Lighting: Backlit moss walls, LED strips behind wood features, pendant fixtures over key areas
  • Task Lighting: Linear track lighting over training zones, adjustable for different activities
  • Overall Temperature: Warm whites (2700K-3000K) - not cool clinical whites

Equipment Integration

Key Principle: Equipment should be displayed like sculptural objects, not hidden. The contrast of black professional equipment against light wood creates visual impact and signals quality.

  • Wall-Mounted Storage: Integrate racks, plate storage, and accessories into wood slat walls - functional and beautiful
  • Organized Display: Dumbbells arranged by weight on black metal racks against wood walls
  • Equipment Brands: Rogue, Eleiko, Concept2 - premium brands that look as good as they perform
  • Color Consistency: All equipment in black or dark tones - no bright colors or mismatched pieces
  • Spacing: Generous space around each piece - not crowded, allows equipment to breathe

Biophilic Elements

Why Biophilic Design Matters for SIGLAP

Research shows natural elements reduce stress, improve recovery, and enhance focus - perfect for a strength + physio studio. This isn't just aesthetic; it's functional wellness design.

  • Living Moss Walls: Backlit preserved moss panels (low maintenance) as statement feature - dramatic, Instagram-worthy, conversation starter
  • Potted Plants: Strategic placement - corners, reception, near windows - Snake plants, Monstera, Fiddle Leaf Fig (easy care, high impact)
  • Natural Light: Maximize window exposure, keep windows unobstructed, light sheer curtains if needed for glare control
  • Natural Materials: Wood, plants, natural light - avoid plastic, fake plants, or synthetic finishes where possible
  • Outdoor Connection: If possible, visual connection to exterior greenery through windows

Branding Touchpoints in Space

  • Primary Signage: SIGLAP logo on wood paneled wall behind reception - dimensional letters (brushed metal or wood), backlit or pin-mounted
  • Wayfinding: Minimal signage integrated into wood features - laser-cut or routed into wood panels
  • Educational Content: Movement science posters in simple black frames on white walls - informative, not motivational
  • Workout Cards: Custom-designed cards with SIGLAP branding, stored in wood holders near training zones
  • Towels: Premium towels in brand colors (charcoal or white), neatly rolled in wood cabinetry
  • Signature Scent: Cedar + eucalyptus (ties to wood aesthetic, promotes focus and recovery) - diffused subtly
  • Retail Display: If selling branded items or supplements, integrate into wood shelving - curated, minimal, premium

Ceiling Treatment

  • Finish: Clean white, not exposed industrial
  • Lighting: Recessed cove lighting around perimeter, linear track lighting over training zones
  • Height: Maintain ceiling height perception - avoid drop ceilings unless necessary for acoustic control
  • Accent: Consider wood slat details on ceiling sections for continuity (like Image 2)

Flooring Details

  • Primary: Light wood or wood-look luxury vinyl plank (LVP) - warm, cohesive, durable
  • Training Zones: Rubber platforms for deadlifts/Olympic lifts - black to match equipment
  • Recovery Area: Consider slightly softer flooring or area rugs for comfort during floor work
  • Consistency: Same flooring throughout (except platforms) for visual continuity

Design NOT-TO-DO List

  • ❌ No exposed concrete or raw industrial finishes
  • ❌ No neon signs or motivational quotes on walls
  • ❌ No mismatched equipment colors (no red, blue, bright accents)
  • ❌ No wall-to-wall mirrors (strategic placement only)
  • ❌ No bright white cool lighting (creates harsh clinical feel)
  • ❌ No clutter or exposed storage chaos
  • ❌ No traditional "gym" aesthetic - this is a wellness studio

Brand Name Options

KINETIC Assessment Summary

Legal Status: No direct trademark conflict in Singapore IPOS registry. Kinetics Climbing Gym exists but in different category. Kinetic X Fitness permanently closed.

Strategic Concerns: Heavily saturated globally (20+ fitness/physio businesses). Requires more SEO investment. Alternative spellings (KINETIK, KYNETIC) NOT recommended due to spelling confusion, unprofessional perception, and SEO issues.

Verdict: Viable with strong differentiator (e.g., "KINETIC Strength Studio"), but more distinctive names offer easier market positioning.

Client Journey Through Technology

Discovery → Inquiry

Channels: Instagram/Facebook ad → Website → WhatsApp or Instagram DM

Tech Flow:

  • Client sees social media ad, clicks through to website
  • Browses services, reads team bios, checks pricing
  • Clicks WhatsApp button or sends Instagram DM
  • Sleekflow auto-reply provides welcome message + booking link
  • Staff follow up via Sleekflow to answer questions

Booking → Payment

Platform: Rezerv.io

Tech Flow:

  • Client clicks Rezerv.io booking link (from website, WhatsApp, or social)
  • Selects service type (Movement Assessment, Strength Program, Recovery)
  • Chooses date/time based on real-time trainer availability
  • Completes payment via Stripe/PayPal integration
  • Receives automated confirmation email + SMS
  • Added to member portal with login credentials

Pre-Session → Attendance

Platforms: Rezerv.io (automated) + Sleekflow (if needed)

Tech Flow:
